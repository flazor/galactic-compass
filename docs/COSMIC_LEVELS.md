Frames of Reference for an Observer on Earth

1. Earth Rotation (~465 m/s at equator)

Scale: Earth diameter = 0.0000000013 light-years (~13,000 km)
Direction: Eastward (toward sunrise)
Period: 24 hours
Discoverer: Leon Foucault (1851) - first direct experimental proof with pendulum at Pantheon
Earlier Theory: Nicolaus Copernicus (1543) proposed Earth's rotation
References: Foucault, L. (1851), Comptes rendus; Copernicus, N. (1543), De revolutionibus

2. Earth's Orbit Around Sun (~30 km/s)

Scale: Orbital diameter = 0.00003 light-years (~2 AU, 300 million km)
Direction: Eastward along ecliptic
Period: 365.25 days
Discoverers: Nicolaus Copernicus (1543) - heliocentric model; Johannes Kepler (1609-1619) - proved elliptical orbits
Earlier Theory: Aristarchus of Samos (~270 BCE)
References: Copernicus (1543), De revolutionibus; Kepler (1609), Astronomia nova; Kepler (1619), Harmonices Mundi

3. Solar System's Galactic Orbit (~220 km/s)

Scale: Galactic orbit diameter = ~52,000 light-years (Sun's orbit around galaxy center)
Direction (Solar Apex): Toward Lyra/Hercules
RA: 18h 49m, Dec: +35.8°
Near: Vega, M13
Period: ~230 million years (orbital) + ~30 million years (galactic plane oscillation)
Discoverer: William Herschel (1783) - solar apex direction; Jan Oort (1927) - galactic rotation
References: Herschel, W. (1783); Oort, J. (1927), Bull. Astron. Inst. Netherlands, 3, 275; Dehnen & Binney (1998), MNRAS, 298, 387
Notes: Solar System oscillates ~27 parsecs above/below galactic plane with period ~33 Myr

4. Milky Way in Local Group (~62 km/s)

Milky Way's peculiar motion toward the Local Group barycenter (on the MW-Andromeda axis)

Scale: Local Group diameter = ~10 million light-years
Direction: Toward Local Group barycenter (near M31)
RA: 00h 45m, Dec: +41.4°
Galactic: l = 121.7°, b = -21.5°
Period: ~4.5 billion years until MW-M31 collision
Discoverer: Vesto Slipher (1912) - detected Andromeda's blueshift
References: Makarov et al. (2025), A&A; van der Marel et al. (2012), ApJ

5. Local Void Push (~259 km/s)

Local Sheet of galaxies repelled away from the underdense Local Void

Scale: Local Void diameter = ~100 million light-years
Direction: Away from Local Void center
RA: 06h 39m, Dec: +1.7°
Galactic: l = 210°, b = -2°
Near: Monoceros/Orion region
Period: N/A (ongoing repulsion)
Discoverer: Brent Tully et al. (2008) - identified void repulsion as major component
References: Tully et al. (2008), ApJ, 676, 184

6. Virgo Cluster Pull (~185 km/s)

Local Group pulled toward the Virgo Cluster (Tully 2008 component)

Scale: Virgo Supercluster diameter = ~110 million light-years
Direction: Toward M87 (Virgo Cluster center, 53 Mly distant)
RA: 12h 31m, Dec: +12.4°
Galactic: l = 283.8°, b = +74.5°
Near: Spica, Virgo A
Period: N/A (ongoing infall)
Discoverer: Marc Aaronson et al. (1982); Tully et al. (2008) quantified component
References: Tully et al. (2008), ApJ, 676, 184; Aaronson et al. (1982), ApJ, 258, 64

7. Large-Scale Flow (~455 km/s)

Combined pull from Great Attractor, Shapley Concentration, and Centaurus structures

Scale: Flow extends to Shapley Concentration at ~650 million light-years
Direction: Toward Centaurus/Great Attractor/Shapley region
RA: 12h 29m, Dec: -47.7°
Galactic: l = 299°, b = +15°
Period: N/A (ongoing flow)
Discoverer: Tully et al. (2008) - decomposed bulk flow; Dressler (1987) - Great Attractor
References: Tully et al. (2008), ApJ, 676, 184; Dressler (1987), ApJ; Tully et al. (2014), Nature, 513, 71

8. CMB Dipole / Cosmic Rest Frame (~370 km/s net)

Net motion relative to Cosmic Microwave Background.
This is the measured TOTAL — the verification target, not an additive component.
The vector sum of levels 1-7 should approximate this value.

Scale: Observable universe diameter = ~93 billion light-years
Direction: Toward Leo/Crater boundary
RA: 11h 12m, Dec: -7°
Galactic: l = 264°, b = +48°
Near: Regulus, Hydra
Discoverers: Edward Conklin (1969) - first detection; Paul Henry (1971) - declination; Brian Corey & David Wilkinson (1976) - confirmation
References: Conklin (1969), Nature, 222, 971; Henry (1971), Nature, 231, 516; Kogut et al. (1993), ApJ, 419, 1; Planck (2020)

---

Decomposition Notes

Levels 5-7 follow the Tully et al. (2008) three-component decomposition of the Local Group's
motion relative to the CMB. These three components are nearly orthogonal, so their magnitudes
add in quadrature (not linearly): sqrt(259² + 185² + 455²) ≈ 560 km/s for the Local Group.

Adding the Sun's motion within the Local Group (levels 1-4) adjusts this to the measured
CMB dipole of ~370 km/s for the Sun.

Key Reference: Tully, R.B., Shaya, E.J., Karachentsev, I.D. et al. (2008), ApJ, 676, 184
