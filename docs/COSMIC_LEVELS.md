Frames of Reference for Earth's Motion

1. Earth Rotation (~465 m/s at equator)

Scale: Earth diameter = 0.0000000013 light-years (~13,000 km)
Direction: Eastward (toward sunrise)
Period: 24 hours
Discoverer: Léon Foucault (1851) - first direct experimental proof with pendulum at Panthéon
Earlier Theory: Nicolaus Copernicus (1543) proposed Earth's rotation
References: Foucault, L. (1851), Comptes rendus; Copernicus, N. (1543), De revolutionibus

2. Earth's Orbit Around Sun (~30 km/s)

Scale: Orbital diameter = 0.00003 light-years (~2 AU, 300 million km)
Direction: Eastward along ecliptic
Period: 365.25 days
Discoverers: Nicolaus Copernicus (1543) - heliocentric model; Johannes Kepler (1609-1619) - proved elliptical orbits
Earlier Theory: Aristarchus of Samos (~270 BCE)
References: Copernicus (1543), De revolutionibus; Kepler (1609), Astronomia nova; Kepler (1619), Harmonices Mundi

3. Solar System's Galactic Orbit (~220 km/s)

Scale: Galactic orbit diameter = ~52,000 light-years (Sun's orbit around galaxy center)
Direction (Solar Apex): Toward Hercules/Cygnus
RA: 18h 28m, Dec: +30°
Near: Vega, M13
Period: ~230 million years
Discoverer: William Herschel (1783)
References: Herschel, W. (1783); Dehnen & Binney (1998), MNRAS, 298, 387

4. Local Group Motion (Andromeda approach, ~120 km/s)

Milky Way + Andromeda + ~50 galaxies falling toward each other

Scale: Local Group diameter = ~10 million light-years
Direction: Toward M31 (Andromeda Galaxy)
RA: 00h 42m, Dec: +41° (2.5 million ly distant)
Near: Triangulum (M33), Mirach
Discoverer: Vesto Slipher (1912) - detected Andromeda's blueshift
References: Slipher (1912); van der Marel et al. (2012), ApJ; Sawala et al. (2024), MNRAS

5. Virgo Cluster Infall (~300 km/s)

Local Group falling toward Virgo Cluster center

Scale: Virgo Supercluster diameter = ~110 million light-years
Direction: Toward M87 (Virgo Cluster center, 53 million ly distant)
RA: 12h 27m, Dec: +12°
Near: Spica, Virgo A
Discoverers: Marc Aaronson et al. (1980-1982), Allan Sandage & Gustav Tammann (1985)
References: Aaronson et al. (1982), ApJ, 258, 64; Tammann & Sandage (1985), ApJ, 294

6. Great Attractor (~600 km/s)

Massive gravity anomaly pulling Virgo Supercluster

Scale: Structure spans ~300-500 million light-years (center at ~150-250 million ly)
Direction: Toward Norma Cluster (ACO 3627)
RA: 16h 15m, Dec: -61°
Discoverer: Alan Dressler (1987)
References: Dressler, A. (1987), ApJ; Lynden-Bell et al. (1988), ApJ, 326, 19; Woudt et al. (2007), MNRAS, 383, 445

7. Shapley Supercluster Pull (~85 km/s additional)

Even larger structure beyond Great Attractor

Scale: Supercluster spans ~300-400 million light-years (center at 650 million ly)
Direction: Toward Shapley Concentration (A3558 core)
RA: 13h 25m, Dec: -31°
Discoverer: Harlow Shapley (1930)
References: Shapley, H. (1930); Raychaudhury, S. (1989), ApJ, 347, 17; Proust et al. (2006), A&A, 447, 133

8. CMB Dipole / Cosmic Rest Frame (~370 km/s net)

Net motion relative to Cosmic Microwave Background
Our ultimate reference frame for "motion through space"

Scale: Observable universe diameter = ~93 billion light-years
Direction: Toward Leo/Crater boundary
RA: 11h 12m, Dec: -7°
Galactic: l = 264°, b = +48°
Near: Regulus, Hydra
Discoverers: Edward Conklin (1969) - first detection; Paul Henry (1971) - declination; Brian Corey & David Wilkinson (1976) - confirmation
References: Conklin (1969), Nature, 222, 971; Henry (1971), Nature, 231, 516; Kogut et al. (1993), ApJ, 419, 1
